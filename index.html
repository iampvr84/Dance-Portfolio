About Me

A dancer shaped by devotion & discipline

Vanshika began her Bharatanatyam journey at the tender age of five under the guidance of her Guru, Smt. Sridevi Jeyaraman. Over the years, she has blossomed into a poised and expressive dancer, captivating audiences at various cultural events and competitions.

For Vanshika, Bharatanatyam is more than an art form — it is a meaningful connection to her heritage, nurturing discipline, strength, and artistic expression. Beyond the stage, she is an active athlete, playing competitive soccer with MYSL – Pumas, and has also trained in Carnatic music, beautifully balancing tradition with her vibrant energy.

Her Arangetram marks a significant milestone, celebrating years of dedication, perseverance, and passion for this timeless art.

"Bharatanatyam is more than just dance — it's my way of staying connected to my roots while building discipline, strength, and artistry."

— Vanshikasri

Guru & School

The Sacred Bond of Guru & Shishya

In Bharatanatyam, the Guru–Shishya relationship is a sacred and timeless bond that forms the very foundation of the art. The Guru is not merely an instructor of technique, but a custodian of a rich and living tradition, carefully passed down through generations.

Through this relationship, the student receives more than choreography — they are immersed in a disciplined journey that nurtures devotion, humility, and a deep understanding of the art's spiritual and cultural essence. Every movement, expression, and rhythm carries the imprint of this lineage, connecting the dancer to something far greater than themselves.

Guru Smt. Sridevi Jeyaraman & Vanshikasri

Guru · Teacher
Smt. Sridevi Jeyaraman

Respectfully addressed as "Sridevi Teacher" by her students, Smt. Sridevi Jeyaraman began her Bharatanatyam training under the guidance of Smt. Vishal Ramani. With deep dedication to the art, she founded Sri Thillai Natya Palli in May 2016, creating a nurturing space for students of all ages to learn and cherish Bharatanatyam.


A visionary mentor, she thoughtfully blends innovation with tradition, introducing creative presentations while preserving the integrity of the classical form. Renowned for her patience, warmth, and keen attention to detail, she instills discipline, confidence, and respect for the art in every student.

The Art Form

What is Bharathanatyam?

One of India's oldest classical dance forms — a sacred offering of body, rhythm, and soul rooted in the temples of Tamil Nadu.

BHA Bhava Emotion · Expression
RA Raga Melody · Music
TA Tala Rhythm · Beat
NATYAM Natya Drama · Dance
01
Nritta
Pure Dance

The abstract, technical dimension — pure movement and footwork with no narrative content. Adavus (rhythmic syllables translated into body geometry), precise angles of limbs, and complex footwork patterns make up Nritta.

02
Nritya
Expressive Dance

The language of emotion — where Abhinaya (expression) brings stories to life through mudras (hand gestures), facial expressions (navarasas), and the movement of eyes. Stories from the Puranas, Ramayana, and devotional poetry are narrated through Nritya.

03
Natya
Dance Drama

The theatrical dimension — where the dancer embodies multiple characters, giving voice to entire epics through costume, expression, and movement. Natya is where dance becomes drama, and the stage becomes a sacred space.

The Traditional Margam — the Path of a Performance
1 Pushpanjali Flower Offering
2 Alarippu Blossoming
3 Jatiswaram Pure rhythm
4 Shabdam Narrative begins
5 Varnam Centrepiece
6 Padam Devotion
7 Tillana Grand finale

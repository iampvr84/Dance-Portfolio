The Margam Path

Traditional Repertoire

A carefully curated sequence of Bharatanatyam pieces that represent the spiritual and physical journey of a dancer, moving from pure rhythm to complex emotional storytelling.

The Invocation

Pushpanjali

An offering of flowers to the Lord of Dance, the Guru, and the audience. This rhythmic salutation sets the stage for the spiritual journey, purifying the space with sacred energy and movement.

Alarippu

A flowering of the body. This piece focuses on pure Nritta (dance) to warm up the limbs and synchronize the eyes and breath with geometric precision.

Melodic Rhythm

Jatiswaram

The convergence of music (Swaras) and rhythmic syllables (Jatis). A vibrant display of complex footwork and sculptural poses without narrative.

Varnam

The most demanding piece in the Margam. It tests the dancer's endurance through intricate rhythm and profound emotional depth (Abhinaya).

Shabdam

Introducing short narratives and mimetic expressions. Typically dedicated to the glory of gods or kings, weaving poetry with gesture.
